City Design - Jaipur

A Sustainable Approach to Address Water Scarcity

City Plan

Jaipur's city plan to address water scarcity involves a combination of modern infrastructure development, community participation, and technology-driven solutions. The city is divided into zones for targeted interventions, ensuring resources are allocated efficiently and effectively.

  • Installing smart water meters across households to monitor usage and minimize waste.
  • Developing centralized reservoirs to store and distribute harvested rainwater.
  • Creating green spaces and permeable surfaces to aid groundwater recharge.
  • Implementing zonal water management strategies to ensure equitable distribution.

SDG Rules

The Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) play a vital role in ensuring the long-term sustainability of water management practices in Jaipur. The following SDGs are particularly relevant to this project:

  • SDG 6 - Clean Water and Sanitation: Ensuring access to safe water for all and managing water resources sustainably.
  • SDG 13 - Climate Action: Addressing the challenges posed by climate change through improved water conservation measures.
  • SDG 11 - Sustainable Cities and Communities: Developing cities that are resilient to climate impacts and use resources efficiently.
  • SDG 3 - Good Health and Well-being: Ensuring that clean water and sanitation are available to improve public health outcomes.
